Hedy Lamarr

Hedy Lamarr's Life

1914

Born as Hedwig Eva Kiesler in Vienna, Austria on November 9, to a curious banker father and a concert pianist mother who nurtured her love of science and the arts.

1933

Married Austrian arms dealer Fritz Mandl; escaped his oppressive control in 1937 and fled to London, bringing with her valuable knowledge of military technology.

1938

Discovered by MGM's Louis B. Mayer in London and brought to Hollywood, where she starred in hits like Algiers, Boom Town, and Samson and Delilah.

1942

Co-invented a frequency-hopping signal system with composer George Antheil to prevent enemy interception of Allied torpedoes; awarded U.S. Patent No. 2,292,387.

1953

Officially became a U.S. citizen and continued acting in films while using her fame to support war bonds and American troops during WWII.

2014

Posthumously inducted into the National Inventors Hall of Fame for her pioneering invention that led to modern wireless technologies like Wi-Fi, GPS, and Bluetooth.
